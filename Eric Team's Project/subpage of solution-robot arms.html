On March 29, 2021, Boston Dynamics announced via a video on their YouTube channel the Stretch robot that was designed for warehouse automation. It has a square mobile base containing a set of wheels, a “perception mast” with cameras and other sensors, and a robotic arm with seven degrees of freedom and a suction pad array on the end that can grab and move boxes up to 23 kilograms (50 lbs) in weight.


Stretch robot offers predictability for warehouse operations, working continuously and keeping the flow of goods moving. With the ability to move hundreds of cases an hour, Stretch ensures that daily goals are met even as order fulfillment demands soar.


Stretch is ready to implement and is up and running in just days from delivery, working within existing warehouse infrastructure. Since the robot makes all decisions in real time, no pre-programming is required before putting Stretch to work.Long battery life powers Stretch through single or multiple shifts any time of day or night. Its computer vision system allows Stretch to detect and automatically retrieve any boxes that shift and fall while unloading, so Stretch can keep going without operator intervention.
